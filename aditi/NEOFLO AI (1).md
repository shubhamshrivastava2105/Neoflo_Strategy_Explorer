# **Ramp vs. Neoflo — Competitive Analysis**

---

## **Framing**

Ramp is the largest, fastest-moving player in our competitive set — but it overlaps with Neoflo only **partially**. Ramp is a card-led, interchange-funded spend platform expanding into AP and procurement. Neoflo is a done-for-you finance-operations service. They collide on AP automation, not on most of what each does. Read everything below with that distinction in mind.

---

## **1\. Company Snapshot**

Ramp is a New York–based fintech founded in 2019 (CEO Eric Glyman), positioned as an all-in-one financial-operations hub combining corporate cards, automated expense tracking, accounts payable, procurement, and treasury services.

Crossed $1B annualized revenue late 2025, is free-cash-flow positive, and has signaled intent to be IPO-ready by end of 2026\.

---

## **2\. Business Model (the part that matters most)**

Ramp's model is fundamentally different from Tipalti / Peakflo / Neoflo:

* The **core card and expense platform is free** — revenue comes from **interchange**.  
* Plus and Enterprise tiers add paid features.  
* The software is cheap/free because Ramp monetizes the card rails underneath.

**Implication:** Ramp is strongest where there is card spend to monetize. Its AP and procurement modules are extensions of a card-led core, not the heritage of the business.

---

## **3\. Product & AI**

**Scope:** expense management, AP automation, corporate cards, procurement, treasury, and AI decision-making.

**AI ("Ramp Intelligence") — the real wedge:**

* In October 2025 alone, the AI made 26M+ decisions across $10B+ in spend.  
* Prevented 511,157 out-of-policy transactions worth \~$291M; swept idle cash into investments; blocked AI-generated fraud.  
* Beta teams report 85% fewer manual reviews, with agents detecting \~15× more policy violations.  
* Targeting a 30× productivity increase by 2027 via parallel AI agents.  
* Design philosophy: "zero click" — collapsing dozen-click workflows toward zero.

**AP specifics:** invoice ingestion, approvals, ACH/wire/check payments; procurement intake, approvals, and contract management; AI that surfaces savings and drafts vendor negotiations.

**International:** historically US-centric, expanding fast — acquired Billhop (UK/EU payments authorization), opened London and Stockholm offices, holds a multi-year Visa issuing agreement, and launched stablecoin-backed cards with Stripe. Nearly half its customers already transact across 180+ countries.

---

## **4\. Clients**

25,000+ companies including Shopify, Glossier, and Anduril — skewing venture-backed startups through enterprise; companies that fit a self-serve, card-led model.

---

## **5\. Where Ramp Beats Neoflo**

* **Scale, momentum, and capital** — $40B valuation, $1B+ revenue, FCF-positive, 25,000+ customers. Nothing else in the set is close.  
* **Free/cheap via interchange** — cards fund the software, a pricing advantage a services model can't match head-on.  
* **AI agent depth at real scale** — tens of millions of automated decisions monthly across $10B+ spend; operational proof most rivals lack.  
* **Native card \+ spend control** — real-time policy enforcement and fraud prevention at the point of purchase.  
* **Fast self-serve onboarding** (\<2 weeks) plus a free entry point.

---

## **6\. Where Neoflo Wins**

* **Ramp is a tool you operate; Neoflo does the work.** Even with "zero-click" agents, the client still owns the platform, the exceptions, and the judgment calls. Neoflo owns the outcome.  
* **Ramp is card-led — the messy non-card work is the seam.** Complex vendor payables, exception-heavy invoices, AR/collections, and month-end close are not its sweet spot.  
* **Breadth across the cash cycle.** Neoflo spans P2P \+ O2C \+ R2R. Ramp is spend-out \+ cards \+ emerging AP; no real receivables/collections engine and no close product.  
* **Fit for non-Ramp-shaped companies.** The interchange model favors card-friendly US/Western businesses; heavy non-card cross-border payables and multi-entity/multi-ERP complexity need a managed layer.  
* **Geography.** Ramp is overwhelmingly US-centric (only now entering UK/EU). India / SEA / cross-border mid-market is open ground.

---

## **7\. Honest Pros & Cons**

**Ramp — pros:** unmatched scale, momentum, and funding; free core via interchange; deepest AI-agent operational track record; native card/spend controls and fraud prevention; fast onboarding; strong logos.

**Ramp — cons:** card-led model fits complex non-card payables poorly; AP/procurement are newer extensions; minimal O2C/AR and no real R2R/close; US-centric; self-serve software (exceptions and operation stay with the client); interchange model needs card spend to monetize.

**Neoflo — pros:** done-for-you managed service; full P2P \+ O2C \+ R2R breadth; outcome/SLA pricing; rails-agnostic; owns exceptions and judgment work; geographic wedge.

**Neoflo — cons:** can't match Ramp's scale, brand, free pricing, or AI-at-scale proof; far earlier stage; no card/interchange revenue engine; higher-stakes "we run it" sell; trust/security gate to clear.

## **9\. Positioning Takeaways vs. Ramp**

1. **Do not fight Ramp on cards or spend management — concede that lane.** Different game; Neoflo is not a card company.  
2. **Compete on the non-card, exception-heavy work Ramp's model deprioritizes** — complex vendor payables, receivables/collections, and the close.  
3. **"Agents you run vs. an operation we run."** Ramp gives teams superhuman agents; Neoflo removes the need to staff or manage them at all.  
4. **Lean on breadth \+ geography** — full cash-cycle ownership and a non-US footprint are both places Ramp is weak today.  
5. **Co-exist, don't displace.** Many prospects will keep Ramp for cards/expense; Neoflo runs the surrounding AP/AR/close operation on top, lowering the trust barrier and avoiding a head-on collision with a $40B incumbent.

### **10\. Future Implementation — How Neoflo Beats Ramp (In Brief)**

**Thesis:** You can't out-Ramp Ramp. Win by attacking the flows its interchange model is structurally disincentivized to serve, and by being the *operation*, not the *tool*.

1. Own non-card payables (wires, cross-border ACH — no interchange for Ramp).  
2. Own the full cash cycle — AR/collections \+ close (R2R) as a service.  
3. Out-price on *value* via the exception flywheel, not sticker.  
4. Win India / SEA / cross-border first.  
5. Integrate with Ramp, don't fight it — turn the incumbent into a channel.  
6. Verticalize where card spend is low (manufacturing, logistics, construction, healthcare, real estate).  
7. Match Ramp's proof discipline with hard, climbing metrics.  
8. Cash-cycle / working-capital intelligence (CFO-level wedge Ramp can't match).

**Avoid:** launching a card, chasing expense management, or competing on "free." Win condition \= non-card payables \+ full cash cycle \+ done-for-you outcomes \+ non-US depth.
