## **2\. Core Distinction (Read This First)**

Peakflo and Neoflo are **not the same kind of product** — and this difference drives every pro/con below.

* **Peakflo \= software you operate.** Your finance team logs in and runs it. AI assists; humans still drive.  
* **Neoflo \= an AI \+ human managed service.** Neoflo owns the work and the outcome, backed by SLAs. Your team receives results.

One-line framing: *Peakflo gives you a tool and leaves the messy 20% to your team. Neoflo owns the whole process end-to-end and only gets paid when the outcome lands.*

### **3.2 Order-to-Cash (O2C) workflow**

* Cash application

* AI voice agent  
* Deduction & dispute management

### **3.3 Record-to-Report (R2R) — partial**

**As of 2026, Peakflo introduced some R2R features — but it has only a few parts of R2R, not the full feature set.**

* Strong: automated reconciliation that syncs to the accounting system  
* Newer / agent-led: accruals, journal entries, variance analysis, close orchestration, close-progress dashboards, audit trails, multi-entity coordination  
* Gap vs. specialists: full consolidation, statutory reporting, intercompany eliminations, dedicated close management (FloQast / BlackLine tier)

## **5\. Where Peakflo Genuinely Beats Neoflo**

* **Self-serve control** — teams that want to own their workflows in-house get a polished, configurable tool.  
* **Product maturity** — years of shipped modules, documented help center, named integrations, public customer base.  
* **SEA collection depth** — multi-channel dunning (WhatsApp / SMS / email / LINE / Zalo), virtual accounts for auto-reconciliation, predictive AI for likely-overdue invoices.  
* **Lower commitment for SMBs** — a software subscription is an earlier "yes" than handing over an entire process.  
* **Vendor self-service portal at scale** — a service layer doesn't replace it.

---

## **6\. Where Neoflo Wins**

* **AI \+ human managed service** with SLA-backed outcomes and usage-based pricing (pay for results; miss an SLA, don't pay full price).  
* **Exceptions are the real work** — Peakflo automates the happy path; mismatched POs, odd invoices, and disputes still land on the customer's staff. Neoflo owns the full cycle *including* exceptions.  
* **Outcomes, not tools** — Neoflo is accountable to the result, not just to providing software.  
* **Faster, lighter onboarding than a BPO:**  
  * Pilot design — **1 week**  
  * Go-live — **4 weeks**  
  * ROI review — **90 days**  
* **R2R coverage** — owns month-end close as a managed outcome ("close in days, not weeks"), where Peakflo's close offering is still partial.

## **7\. Honest Cons of Each**

### **Peakflo — cons**

* Staff still run it day-to-day.  
* Exceptions remain the customer's problem.  
* SMB / mid-market focused.  
* Small funding base.

### **Neoflo — cons**

* New, with a thinner public track record.  
* Buyers who specifically want to own software in-house won't be a fit.  
* Data/security trust hurdle — an outside party touching the whole process triggers heavier scrutiny (security questionnaires, SOC 2 / ISO 27001 proof, access controls, VPC deployment). *Mitigated by Neoflo's existing certifications and VPC option.*

## **9\. Positioning Takeaways**

1. **Don't claim Peakflo "can't" do something** (payments, close, R2R) — they'll demo it and you lose credibility. Compete on *how* it's delivered: a tool your team runs vs. an outcome Neoflo owns.  
2. **Lead with exception ownership and SLA accountability** — this is the cleanest, most durable wedge.  
3. **Neutralize the trust objection early** — present SOC 2 Type II, ISO 27001, GDPR/CCPA, and VPC deployment before the security team asks.  
4. **R2R is contested, not open ground** — frame it as "managed close outcome vs. another platform to staff," especially against close specialists.

## **10\. Future Roadmap — How Neoflo Becomes Better Than Peakflo (In Brief)**

**Guiding principle:** Don't out-feature Peakflo as software. Win by leaning into what a managed service can do that software structurally cannot.

1. **Exception-intelligence flywheel** — turn every human-resolved exception into AI training data so each client's touchless % climbs over time. Publish exception-resolution SLAs by category. *(This is the core moat.)*  
2. **Client control tower (the one software surface to build)** — real-time visibility into every invoice, payment, and close task \+ live SLA tracking. Clients *watch* the work; Neoflo *does* it.  
3. **Kill the trust objection** — shadow/parallel-run mode before cutover, auditable payment controls (maker-checker, segregation of duties), public Trust Center, SOC 1 \+ SOC 2 Type II \+ ISO 27001, VPC-by-default, hard ROI proof and references.  
4. **Full R2R-as-a-service** — sell the *closed books* (recs \+ accruals \+ JE \+ flux \+ consolidation \+ statutory \+ audit-ready workpapers) with a "books closed by day X" SLA. Beats Peakflo (partial) and FloQast/BlackLine (self-run).  
5. **Rails-agnostic payments** — orchestrate the client's existing banking rails instead of being the rail. Makes Neoflo geography-agnostic and sidesteps Peakflo's licensing constraints.  
6. **Verticalized AI agents \+ skill memory** — domain-tuned agents per industry; retain institutional knowledge so it doesn't walk out when staff quit; add managed voice/collections calling.  
7. **Outcome pricing \+ data network effects** — price on results (per resolved exception, % DSO reduction, early-pay discounts captured); offer cross-client benchmarking, vendor-risk intelligence, and network-level fraud/duplicate detection.

**Do NOT:** chase Peakflo's dunning breadth, vendor-portal polish, or ERP-connector count as the strategy. Match table stakes only. Win condition \= **outcome ownership \+ trust \+ the exception flywheel**, not a longer feature list.
